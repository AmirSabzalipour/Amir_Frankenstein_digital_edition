Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley - Frankenstein

Welcome to the mini digital edition of Mary Shelley's Frankenstein.

This project was created for Text as Data II, a course that is part of the Master of Digital Text Analysis at the Faculty of Arts, University of Antwerp, during the 2024-2025 academic year. On this website, I have created a digital representation of the book.

Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus is an 1818 Gothic novel by Mary Shelley, telling the story of Victor Frankenstein, a scientist who creates a sentient being in an unorthodox experiment. Shelley began writing it at the age of 18, and the first edition was published anonymously in 1818. Her name appeared for the first time in the second edition, published in 1821.

The novel was inspired by Shelleyâ€™s travels in 1815 near Frankenstein Castle in Germany and her time in Geneva, Switzerland, where the story is set. Discussions about galvanism and the occult with her future husband, Percy Bysshe Shelley, further shaped her ideas.

Blending Gothic and Romantic elements, Frankenstein has become a cornerstone of English literature, profoundly influencing the horror genre and popular culture. The manuscript has been digitized and made available online by the Bodleian Library and is part of the Shelley-Godwin Archive, which served as the basis for this digital edition.
